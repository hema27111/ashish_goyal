About me

I am passionate about biology, mathematics and computer, and their amalgamation to carry out research. My research focuses on applying mathematical, statistical and computational techniques to biological and medical related problems. Being interdisciplinary in nature, I employ dynamical modelling including ordinary, delay, stochastic and partial differential equations. Optimization techniques are also used extensively to estimate unknown parameters and to find optimal solutions. These mathematical models and techniques are widely used and have previously proved to be powerful tools for describing any biological, epidemiological, social, or ecological phenomena. I have applied these techniques to epidemiological and in vivo problems related to hepatitis B virus (HBV), hepatitis C virus (HCV) and hepatitis D virus (HDV). Currently, I am developing new temporal-spatial models of HCV. Within that context, I have developed a good understanding of the interaction between a viral infection and the immune system, as well as the spread of an infection through a community.​​
